Next steps:

Obtain a point-of-care urine drug screen

If UDS negative for fentanyl/methadone/opiates/oxycodone AND criteria for "minimal or no fentanyl dependence" are met, administer Vivitrol.

If UDS positive for fentanyl/methadone/opiates/oxycodone, do NOT administer. Further discussion needed on degree of opioid dependence. Consult provider.

---

When to notify provider:

Consult provider if unable to obtain UDS, if UDS is positive for fentanyl/methadone/opiates/oxycodone, or if patient does not meet "minimal or no fentanyl dependence" criteria.

Do not administer and consult provider if patient does not meet criteria for "minimal or no fentanyl dependence", see definition below.

Patient has any signs of opioid depedency: more than minimal depedency, need to use opioids to avoid withdrawals, unable to go without opioids.

Do not rely only on urine drug tests as they can be falsely negative; notify provider if there is suspicion of opioid dependency.
